A grand festival of Kerala celebrating the return of King Mahabali.

Durga Puja

A major festival in Bengal celebrating Goddess Durga.

Navratri

A Hindu festival dedicated to the worship of Goddess Durga.

India’s regional festivals are a vibrant expression of local culture, traditions, and community spirit, uniting people in celebration and devotion. These festivals, deeply rooted in history and folklore, showcase the diverse cultural heritage of different states while spreading joy, togetherness, and spirituality.

Onam, the grandest festival of Kerala, is a ten-day-long harvest festival that marks the legendary homecoming of King Mahabali. The celebrations include elaborate floral rangolis called Pookalam, the famous Vallam Kali (boat races), traditional dance forms like Kathakali and Pulikali, and the grand Onam Sadhya—a feast served on banana leaves with an array of delicious vegetarian dishes. The festival reflects Kerala’s cultural richness and the spirit of harmony among its people.

Durga Puja, predominantly celebrated in West Bengal, is one of the most elaborate and artistically vibrant festivals in India. The festival honors Goddess Durga’s victory over the demon Mahishasura, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil. Magnificently crafted idols of the goddess are worshipped in grandly decorated pandals, accompanied by cultural performances, processions, and traditional Dhunuchi dance. The festival is not only a religious occasion but also a major social and artistic event, drawing visitors from across the country.

Navratri, celebrated across India, is a nine-night festival dedicated to the worship of Goddess Durga in her different forms. The festival is marked by fasting, devotional songs, and vibrant dance forms like Garba and Dandiya, especially in Gujarat and Maharashtra. People dress in colorful traditional attire, gathering in large groups to perform rhythmic dances, creating a festive and spiritual atmosphere.
