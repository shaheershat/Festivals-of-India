Republic Day

Celebrated on January 26th to honor the Constitution of India.

Independence Day

Celebrated on August 15th to mark India's independence from British rule.

Gandhi Jayanti

Celebrated on October 2nd to honor Mahatma Gandhi's birthday.

National festivals in India are deeply rooted in the country’s history, symbolizing its journey towards freedom, democracy, and unity. These occasions not only commemorate significant historical events but also instill a sense of patriotism and national pride among citizens.

Republic Day, celebrated annually on January 26th, marks the adoption of India's Constitution in 1950, signifying the nation’s transition into a sovereign democratic republic. The highlight of the day is the grand parade in New Delhi, which showcases the country's military strength, cultural heritage, and technological advancements.

Independence Day, observed on August 15th, honors India's freedom from British rule in 1947. The day begins with the Prime Minister hoisting the national flag at the Red Fort in Delhi, followed by a powerful speech addressing the nation.

Gandhi Jayanti, observed on October 2nd, is dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi, the Father of the Nation, who played a pivotal role in India's struggle for independence through his principles of non-violence and truth.
